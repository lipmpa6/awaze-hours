Key findings
✓ Story is reassuring overall
Across the full period, Beyond prices +3.4% above Awaze on average. The near-term discount is a feature, not a bug — dTBA and dOBP intentionally reduce prices close-in to drive conversion, recovering margin as lead time extends.
⚡ May looks aggressive — −23% vs Awaze
The May 2026 gap is the widest in the dataset at −23% vs Awaze's working price. This is partly expected given we've just gone live and dTBA discounts are steepest in the first few weeks, but worth monitoring closely in the first fortnight.
📈 Apr 2027 crossover — far-out premiums kick in
From April 2027, Beyond's working price runs +12–14% above midline and +6–12% above Awaze. This is driven by the far-out tier / dTBA premium step that typically engages ~320–350 days out. As noted, these may be calibrated down slightly after go-live.
✓ Sep/Oct 2026 crossover point
Beyond crosses above Awaze's working price in September 2026, running +10–16% higher through Q4 2026. This is the period where Beyond's dynamic model starts to pull ahead — useful evidence for the POC narrative.
⚡ Jan 2027 dip vs Awaze
January 2027 shows Beyond −12% vs Awaze working price — the only mid/far term month where Beyond is materially lower. Consistent with the Jan 2027 midline finding (median −17.8% in the midlines analysis). Likely a seasonality calibration issue to investigate.
📊 Working price consistently above midline from Oct 26
From October 2026 onward, Beyond's working price runs 6–19% above its own midline every month. This demonstrates the compounding effect of Beyond's dynamic factors on top of the agreed midlines — the value-add beyond a static pricing approach.

What the corridor means. The corridor defines the range within which Beyond's working price can move relative to the midline: no lower than −20% (the floor) and no higher than +20% (the ceiling). Hitting the corridor boundary is not an error — it is the system operating within agreed constraints. The goal is for working prices to sit comfortably within the corridor the vast majority of the time, moving dynamically in response to demand without needing to press against either bound.
Headline finding. Across 1.1M listing-weeks, 94.1% sit inside the tight corridor (−20% to +20%). The +20% ceiling is breached in just 1.9% of listing-weeks, peaking at 7.5% in January 2028. Floor breaches are almost entirely a go-live artefact — 81% in May, resolving rapidly through June and July, and effectively zero from October 2026 onwards.

On the May question: Yes — floor hits are heavily front-loaded within May. The first two weeks (w/c May 4 and May 11) see 98% and 96% floor hit rates respectively, with averages of around −39% vs midline. By the week of May 18 this drops to 75%, and the final week of May (May 25) is down to 57%. The dTBA close-in discount is steepest for the nearest weeks and reduces as the booking horizon extends — exactly as expected.

Answers the question: is May front-loaded?
Yes — heavily front-loaded. The first two weeks of May see ~97–98% floor hit rates, with working prices averaging −39% below midline. This is the dTBA close-in discount at its most extreme, applying to dates that are days away. By week 3 it drops to 75% and week 4 to 57%, as the booking horizon for those dates extends slightly. This is entirely expected behaviour — the discount is a function of lead time, not a systematic calibration error.
